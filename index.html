Brief Bio

I was born in June 1987 in Athens, Greece. I completed high school and was admitted to the School of Electronic and Computer Engineering at Technical University of Crete (TUC). I obtained the Diploma (5-year Bachelor's program) degree and the MSc. Degree in Electronic and Computer Engineering in October 2011 and in July 2014 respectively, under the guidance of Assistant Professor Aggelos Bletsas. I got my Ph.D. degree from the Electrical and Computer Engineering department of the University of Washington under the supervision of Prof. Matt Reynolds.

Currently, I am working as a Sr. Applications Engineer with the Integrated Circuits Department of Cirtec Medical.

Areas of Interest

  • Integrated systems for wireless neural/EMG signals acquisition .
  • High-rate, Low-power Backscatter Communication systems.
  • RF/microwave systems.
  • Wireless Sensor Networks.
  • Low-cost hardware/firmware development for WSN transceivers.
  • Printed circuit board design for RF applications.
